History of Agra

Agra is a city located in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, famous for its rich history and magnificent monuments, especially the Taj Mahal.

Etymology

The name "Agra" has its roots in ancient history, with its earliest mention in the Mahabharata as "Agravana," meaning "the border of the forest" ('Agra' meaning 'front' or 'border' and 'vana' meaning 'forest'). During the medieval period, particularly under Mughal rule when Persian was the court language, the name likely evolved into its present form, reflecting the linguistic practices of that era. Some scholars suggest an alternative origin from the Sanskrit word "Agar," meaning "saltpan," though this theory is less commonly accepted. Over centuries, the name "Agravana" was simplified to "Agra," mirroring the natural linguistic evolution influenced by various cultures and languages.

Demography

Agra, located in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is a bustling city with a diverse and vibrant population. As of the latest census data, Agra has a population of over 1.5 million people. The city exhibits a rich tapestry of cultural and religious diversity, with Hindus constituting the majority, followed by significant Muslim, Jain, and Christian communities.
The city is also known for its young population, with a significant percentage under the age of 30, contributing to its dynamic workforce. Agra's literacy rate is relatively high, reflecting its numerous educational institutions, including schools, colleges, and universities. The primary languages spoken are Hindi and Urdu, with English also widely used, especially in business and tourism.
Agra's economy is diverse, encompassing tourism, leather goods manufacturing, handicrafts, and marble inlay work, all of which are significant contributors to the city's employment and income. The blend of historical significance, cultural diversity, and economic activity makes Agra a unique and important city in India's demographic landscape

History and Administration

Agra's history spans several centuries and various dynasties, beginning with its mention as 'Agravana' in the Mahabharata. It was established as a modern city by Sikandar Lodi in 1504 and became a significant Mughal city after Babur's conquest in 1526. Under Akbar, Agra became the Mughal capital, where he built the Agra Fort. Shah Jahan later constructed the iconic Taj Mahal. The city experienced decline after Aurangzeb moved the capital to Delhi. Agra came under Maratha control in the 18th century before being taken over by the British in 1803, remaining under their rule until India's independence in 1947. Today, Agra is a major tourist destination known for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri. Administratively, Agra is governed by the Agra Municipal Corporation (AMC) for local civic services, while the District Magistrate oversees law and order and revenue collection. The Divisional Commissioner coordinates regional administration, with broader oversight from the Uttar Pradesh state government and certain central government agencies managing historical sites. The Agra Police Commissionerate ensures public safety, and the Agra Development Authority (ADA) handles urban planning and infrastructure development.

Agriculture

Agriculture plays a vital role in Agra's economy, supported by its fertile soil and favorable climate. The region benefits from the alluvial plains of the Yamuna River, which provide rich agricultural land. Major crops grown in Agra include wheat, paddy, maize, and barley, with wheat being the predominant crop due to the region's favorable conditions for its cultivation. In addition to cereals, Agra also produces significant quantities of pulses, oilseeds, and a variety of vegetables.
Horticulture is another important aspect of Agra's agriculture, with crops such as guava, mango, and pomegranate being widely cultivated. The city is also known for its floriculture, with marigold, rose, and other ornamental flowers grown extensively for both local markets and export. Additionally, Agra's agriculture includes the cultivation of cash crops like cotton and sugarcane, which contribute to the local economy.
Irrigation plays a crucial role in Agra's agricultural productivity, with the Yamuna River and an extensive network of canals providing essential water resources. Traditional farming methods are gradually being complemented by modern techniques, including the use of high-yielding variety seeds, chemical fertilizers, and advanced irrigation systems. Government initiatives and agricultural extension services also support farmers by providing training and resources to improve crop yields and sustainability.
Overall, agriculture in Agra is a mix of traditional practices and modern advancements, contributing significantly to the livelihoods of its rural population and the overall economy of the region.

Famous Things

Agra is renowned for a multitude of attractions and cultural treasures that draw visitors from around the world. Here are some of the most famous things associated with Agra.

  • Monuments and Historical Sites
    1. Taj Mahal: An iconic symbol of love, this white marble mausoleum was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
    2. Agra Fort: A massive red sandstone fort built by Akbar, this UNESCO World Heritage Site served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors.
    3. Fatehpur Sikri: An ancient city built by Akbar, it is another UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning architecture and historical significance.
    4. Itimad-ud-Daulah's Tomb: Often referred to as the "Baby Taj," this Mughal mausoleum was the first in India to be entirely made of marble.
  • Cultural and Artistic Heritage
    1. Pietra Dura Inlay Work: Agra is famous for its intricate marble inlay work, often seen in the Taj Mahal. Artisans create beautiful designs using precious and semi-precious stones.
    2. Leather Goods: The city is known for its high-quality leather products, including shoes, bags, and accessories.
    3. Handicrafts and Carpets: Agra’s bazaars are filled with a variety of handcrafted items and beautifully woven carpets.
  • Cuisine
    1. Petha: A traditional sweet made from ash gourd, often flavored with saffron or rose water.
    2. Mughlai Cuisine: Agra is famous for its rich and flavorful Mughlai dishes, including kebabs, biryanis, and kormas.
    3. Dalmoth: A spicy lentil mix that is a popular snack in Agra.
